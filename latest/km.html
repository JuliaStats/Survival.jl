Kaplan-Meier estimator

The Kaplan-Meier estimator is a nonparametric estimator of the survivor function, i.e. the probability of survival beyond a given time.

The estimate is given by $\hat{S}(t) = \prod_{i: t_i < t} \left( 1 - \frac{d_i}{n_i} \right)$ where $d_i$ is the number of observed events at time $t_i$ and $n_i$ is the number of subjects at risk for the event just before time $t_i$.

The pointwise standard error of the log of the survivor function can be computed using Greenwood's formula: $\text{SE}(\log \hat{S}(t)) = \sqrt{\sum_{i: t_i < t} \frac{d_i}{n_i (n_i - d_i)}}$
